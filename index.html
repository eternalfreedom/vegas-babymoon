No timed entry reservation needed June 1–Sept 30. $20/vehicle at the booth. Drive the one-way loop at your own pace — mostly an air-conditioned car experience. Pull off at Calico Hills overlook (most photogenic, flat 3-min walk from the car to the viewpoint), Red Rock Wash, and White Rock Springs. The Mob Museum — Downtown Las Vegas
~15-min Lyft from the Strip. Housed in the actual 1933 federal courthouse where mob hearings shook the nation — this is the most underrated attraction in Las Vegas. Interactive exhibits on organized crime history, law enforcement, and Las Vegas's rise. Fascinating, fully air-conditioned, ~2 hrs. ~$35/person. Open until 9pm. The building alone is worth the trip. Most Strip tourists never make it downtown — this is the one reason to go.
~$35/person · Fully indoor AC♡ Fascinating, no exertion

♡ Babymoon Essentials
♡
Avoid casino smoke: Route through the Palazzo tower and Grand Canal Shoppes rather than the casino floor. Ask hotel staff for the tower elevator path — it bypasses the casino entirely.
♡
Hydration: Vegas air is ~5% humidity — far drier than Toronto. Pregnancy increases fluid needs. Keep a large water bottle everywhere, especially in the rental car at Red Rock.
♡
June heat: Outdoor time only before 10am or after 6pm. Even a 5-min Strip walk at noon in June can be 105°F with brutal UV. Rideshare freely — this is the right call, not a compromise.
♡
Soaking tub: The Palazzo suite has a deep soaking tub. Use it every evening — excellent for leg and back relief over 5 nights. Pack bath salts if you like them.
♡
Flying note: Pressurized cabin is fine. The return (23:25) is a very late departure — business class flat-bed is the saving grace here. Hydrate extra on both flights.
♡
Nearest hospital: Sunrise Hospital & Medical Center, 3186 S Maryland Pkwy — ~10 min from the Strip. Store the address. Not something you'll need, just smart to have.
💡 Things Most People Miss
💡
Mott 32 Peking duck: Pre-order it when you book (24 hrs in advance required). It's carved tableside and is the standout dish. Don't show up hoping to order it without the heads-up.
💡
Bellagio Conservatory timing: The morning (9–10am) is the quietest time — fewer people, better photos, more peaceful. Going on Day 2 at 9:30am is ideal.
💡
Forum Shops Fall of Atlantis: Free, hourly from noon. The animatronics are campy and spectacular. The 12pm or 1pm show has the lightest crowd.
💡
Venetian gondola — Thursday timing: Goes mid-afternoon on Day 3 (Thu). Weekday afternoons have the shortest wait. Weekend evenings have lineups. The indoor canal ride is genuinely romantic — most guests walk past it.
💡
Peter Luger — Vegas accepts cards: The Brooklyn original is cash-only. The Caesars Palace Vegas location takes all major credit cards. No ATM scramble needed.
💡
Mob Museum speakeasy: The Underground Speakeasy & Distillery is in the Mob Museum basement — a Prohibition-era speakeasy with craft cocktails and mocktails. Worth stopping in for a mocktail for Harriet after the exhibits.
💡
Bellagio Fountains best pairing: "Time to Say Goodbye" (Andrea Bocelli) at the 8pm Saturday show is considered the most moving. The Saturday noon show is a completely different, sunlit experience — catching both on Day 5 is the move.
