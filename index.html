Hotel lobby tour — free, beautiful, effortless
Starting from The Palazzo · Lyft or walk between each
The grandest hotels on the Strip have spectacular lobbies and public art that anyone can walk into — no room key, no ticket, no casino required. Suggested self-guided route (all within 1 mile of each other):

Wynn Las Vegas (3131 Las Vegas Blvd) — the atrium lobby is filled floor-to-ceiling with hand-crafted floral installations. Genuinely one of the most beautiful interiors in the city. Walk through the Wynn Esplanade boutiques at your own pace.

The Cosmopolitan (3708 Las Vegas Blvd) — three-storey chandelier bar in the middle of the property. Each floor has rotating art installations. Level 3 has the Chandelier Bar — sit, have a mocktail, and admire the ceiling-to-floor bead curtains (3 million crystals).

ARIA Resort (3730 Las Vegas Blvd) — one of the most significant hotel art collections in the world. Free to walk through: Maya Lin's Silver River (a silver sculpture of the Colorado River hanging from the lobby ceiling), Jenny Holzer LED text installations, Spencer Finch's Nevada light piece. Takes 20 min and most people walk past all of it.
All free · No casino required · AC throughout♡ Calm, beautiful, effortless

3655 Las Vegas Blvd S, Paris Las Vegas · (702) 944-4224
A French bistro with an outdoor Strip-facing patio — one of the best lunch seats in Vegas. You're looking directly at the Bellagio fountains from your table. Classic French bistro menu: French onion soup, croque monsieur, steak frites, salade niçoise. All pregnancy-safe, relaxed pacing, great service. The Paris Hotel itself is worth a look — the miniature Eiffel Tower and French cobblestone interior are a fun final Vegas moment. No reservation needed for Sunday lunch; just walk in.

♡ Babymoon Essentials
♡
Avoid casino smoke: Use the Palazzo tower elevator and Grand Canal Shoppes path — bypasses the casino floor entirely. Ask hotel staff for the tower route on arrival.
♡
Hydration: Vegas air is ~5% humidity — far drier than Toronto. Pregnancy increases fluid needs. Large water bottle everywhere, especially in the rental car.
♡
June heat: 100–105°F by midday. All outdoor activity before 10am or after 6pm. Rideshare freely — never feel bad about a Lyft in June heat while pregnant.
♡
Soaking tub: Palazzo suite has a deep tub. Use it every evening — great for leg and back relief over 5 nights.
♡
Nearest hospital: Sunrise Hospital & Medical Center — 3186 S Maryland Pkwy, ~10 min from Strip. Save the address. Just smart to have.
💡 Things Most People Miss
💡
Mott 32 Peking duck: Must be pre-ordered 24 hrs ahead when booking. Carved tableside. The best Peking duck in Las Vegas — don't skip it.
💡
Shark Reef jellyfish room: Most visitors rush through it. The jellyfish exhibit is lit beautifully — spend 15 min just standing and watching. Genuinely hypnotic.
💡
Mob Museum speakeasy: The Underground is in the basement — Prohibition-era bar with real craft mocktails. Worth 20 min after the exhibits.
💡
Bellagio bridge: The footbridge spanning the lake gives the straight-on view of the entire fountain spread. Arrive 5 min early on the 8pm Bocelli show — it draws a crowd on Saturdays.
💡
DTF is at ARIA, not Venetian: 3730 Las Vegas Blvd S (ARIA Resort). A short Lyft or 12-min walk south from the Palazzo. Don't show up at the Venetian looking for it.
