♡ Babymoon Essentials
♡
Avoid casino smoke: Route through hotel towers and shopping arcades, not the casino floor. The Venetian/Palazzo has clear tower routes — ask hotel staff for the smoke-free path from your room to the Strip.
♡
Hydration is critical: Vegas air is ~5% humidity — drier than any Canadian city. Pregnancy increases fluid needs. Keep a large water bottle with you everywhere, especially in the car at Red Rock.
♡
June heat: Do all outdoor activity before 10am or after 6pm. Midday heat hits 105°F+ with brutal UV. Rideshare freely — no pride about walking the Strip in that heat, especially while pregnant.
♡
Prenatal pillow: Pack a small pregnancy wedge pillow for 5 nights of hotel sleep. The Palazzo soaking tub is excellent for leg and back relief — use it every evening.
♡
Sauna + steam rooms: Off-limits during pregnancy. Canyon Ranch staff are experienced — mention the pregnancy and they will guide you to appropriate treatments without any awkwardness.
♡
Flying note: Pressurized cabin is fine for pregnancy. The late return flight (23:25) is long — business class flat-bed makes it manageable. Hydrate extra on both flights.
♡
Nearest hospital: Sunrise Hospital & Medical Center, 3186 S Maryland Pkwy — ~10 min from the Strip. Store the address before you go. Not something you'll need, but smart to have.
💡 What Most People Miss
💡
Mott 32 Peking duck: Must be ordered 24 hours ahead when making your reservation. This is the best Peking duck in Las Vegas — carved tableside. Don't miss it; mention it when you call to book.
💡
Bellagio fountain timing: "Time to Say Goodbye" (Andrea Bocelli, ~8pm) is the most emotional and visually spectacular pairing. The daytime shows are beautiful, but the lit-up night version is in a different league entirely.
💡
Venetian gondola — go Thursday: The indoor Grand Canal gondola gets crowded on weekends. Going Jun 4 (Thursday) mid-afternoon means minimal wait and a quieter, more intimate experience.
💡
Red Rock Canyon best stop: The Calico Hills pull-off is the most photogenic by a significant margin. A flat 5-min walk from the parking area reaches the first overlook — worth it before 8am when it's cool and quiet.
💡
Peter Luger — Vegas accepts cards: The legendary Brooklyn original is cash-only, but the Caesars Palace Vegas location accepts all major credit cards. No ATM scramble needed.
💡
High Roller board at 15:30: Starting in daylight and ending in the golden/neon hour gives you both views in one 30-min ride. Far better than boarding fully after dark.
💡
Palazzo tower path: Your hotel room is in the Palazzo tower. You can reach Mott 32, DTF, the Grand Canal Shoppes, and the casino exits entirely via tower corridors and shopping levels — no casino floor required, minimal smoke exposure.
🚁 Helicopter — What to Know
🚁
Strip Night Flight vs Grand Canyon: For a babymoon, the Strip Night Flight (~12 min, ~$100/person) is the right choice. Grand Canyon West Rim tours (~4 hrs) are spectacular but exhausting, involve landing in 105°F desert heat, and are too demanding for this trip. Save the canyon for a future visit.
🚁
Maverick vs Papillon: Maverick Helicopters (maverickhelicopter.com) is preferred for Strip flights — newer Airbus fleet, larger wrap-around windows, smoother boarding process. Both are licensed and reputable.
🚁
Weigh-in: All passengers are weighed for seating configuration (weight & balance). This is standard aviation procedure, handled professionally and privately. Arrive 30 min early as instructed.
🚁
OB clearance: At low-level Strip altitude, cabin pressure is not a concern. Maverick has safely flown thousands of pregnant passengers. Still, get verbal OB sign-off before booking — keep it in writing if your doctor has any concerns about the trimester.
🚁
Hotel pickup: Request Palazzo pickup when booking. No rideshare needed that night — they come to you. Confirm pickup time and meeting point when they confirm your reservation.
