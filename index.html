Wednesday, June 3
Conservatory · Fountains · High Roller · Mott 32 · Gondola
A full day of iconic Vegas sights — free gardens, the fountain show, skyline views at dusk, a world-class dinner, and a romantic gondola ride, all without setting foot on a casino floor.
AM
FHR Breakfast — charged to room
Daily breakfast for two included (min. $60 USD). Must be charged to room to credit at checkout. Sleep in — first full day, no pressure.
✦ FHR: daily breakfast ×2
09:30
Bellagio Conservatory & Botanical Garden
Free, 5-min Lyft. A 14,000 sq ft indoor floral installation — one of the most genuinely beautiful things you can do in Las Vegas, and nearly everyone walks past it. Morning (9–10am) is the quietest time. No casino floor required. Spend 30–45 unhurried minutes here.
Free · Quietest in the morning♡ Peaceful, indoors, AC
10:30
Forum Shops at Caesars — browse & Fall of Atlantis
Walk from Bellagio into Caesars. Enormous air-conditioned Roman-themed mall with barrel-vaulted ceilings, talking statues, and dramatic fountains. Free "Fall of Atlantis" animatronic show runs every hour from noon — campy and spectacular. Great spot to rest and people-watch.
Free Atlantis show hourly from noon
12:30
Lunch near Caesars / Bellagio
Nobu at Caesars is excellent (stick to cooked items for Harriet), or try the Paris Hotel's brasserie for a change of scenery. Take your time — the fountain show doesn't start until 3pm on weekdays.
15:00
Bellagio Fountains — first show of the day
Weekday shows start at 3pm (every 30 min until 8pm, then every 15 min until midnight). 1,200 nozzles, jets 27 storeys high. Best view from the Bellagio bridge. Stay for 2–3 songs — each one is a different piece of music. Note: Saturday fountains start at noon, and the 8pm "Time to Say Goodbye" (Bocelli) is the crown jewel — both on Day 5.
Free · Every 30 min from 3pm weekdays
16:30
High Roller Observation Wheel — twilight boarding
World's tallest observation wheel (550 ft). 30-min rotation in a fully enclosed, air-conditioned gondola. Board at 4:30pm so the ride starts in golden hour and ends at dusk — you see the Strip in both light and neon. Zero exertion, pregnancy-safe. LINQ is a 5-min Lyft from Bellagio.
♡ Enclosed, AC, zero effort30-min ride · Spectacular at dusk

Dinner — Mott 32 (inside The Venetian)

Order: 42-day Peking duck (must request 24 hrs when booking), Black Truffle XO Scallop dumplings, Iberico pork BBQ. All cooked, no raw items. Book via OpenTable; mention pregnancy.
⚑ OpenTable — pre-order duck 24 hrs ahead♡ All cooked, no raw

~$30/person · Indoor AC♡ Gentle, safe, romantic

Thursday, June 4
Shark Reef · Mob Museum · Ding Tai Fung · Mat Franco
The most relaxed full day — a long unhurried morning at the aquarium, Vegas's most underrated museum, dumplings at your doorstep, and a world-class magic show to cap the night.
AM
FHR Breakfast — leisurely start
Charge to room. No early alarm. Today is deliberately unhurried — the aquarium and museum are both open all day.

10:30
Shark Reef Aquarium — Mandalay Bay
~20-min Lyft to the south Strip. Over 2,000 animals — sharks, rays, sea turtles, jellyfish — in a gorgeous fully-indoor aquarium. Give yourself 2+ hours here: the underwater tunnel (sharks swimming overhead) is the centrepiece, the jellyfish room is otherworldly, and the touch pool is worth 20 minutes on its own. Dim, cool, quiet — one of the most genuinely calm and beautiful things you can do in Vegas. ~$30/person.
~$30/person · 2+ hours♡ Calm, dim, fully indoors, zero exertion
13:00
Lunch at Mandalay Bay / head back up the Strip
Grab lunch at Mandalay Bay (Libertine Social is great) or Lyft back toward mid-Strip and eat near the hotel. Slow afternoon ahead.
14:30
The Mob Museum — Downtown Las Vegas
~15-min Lyft from the Strip. Housed in the actual 1933 federal courthouse where mob hearings were held — the most underrated attraction in Vegas. Interactive exhibits on organized crime, law enforcement, and how Las Vegas was literally built by the mob. Fully indoor, air-conditioned, ~2 hours. ~$35/person. Don't miss: the Underground Speakeasy in the basement (mocktails for Harriet from a Prohibition-era bar). Most Strip tourists never make it here — this is worth the Lyft.
~$35/person · Fully indoor💡 Try the speakeasy mocktails downstairs

Return to hotel — freshen up

⚑ Collect rental car tonight for Red Rock tomorrow

5-min walk from your room. World-famous XLB soup dumplings, shrimp fried rice, garlic green beans, wonton soup. All cooked — pregnancy-safe. Confirm MSG preference with server. Eat by 6:30pm to make the show comfortably.
♡ All cooked — confirm MSG with server5-min walk from room

Red Rock Canyon — 13-mile Scenic Loop Drive
No timed entry needed June 1–Sept 30. $20/vehicle. Drive the one-way loop at your own pace — this is a car-based experience with brief stops at viewpoints. Calico Hills pull-off is the most photogenic (flat 3-min walk to the overlook). Red Rock Wash and White Rock Springs are worth stepping out for. The crimson sandstone formations are genuinely unlike anything in Ontario. Stay in AC between stops — back in the car by 11:15am.
No entry reservation in June · $20/vehicle♡ Stay in AC car, flat paved overlooks only

♡ Babymoon Essentials
♡
Avoid casino smoke: Use the Palazzo tower elevator and Grand Canal Shoppes path — you can reach most hotel amenities without crossing the casino floor. Ask staff for the tower route.
♡
Hydration: Vegas air is ~5% humidity — far drier than Toronto. Pregnancy increases fluid needs. Bring a large water bottle everywhere, especially in the rental car.
♡
June heat: 100–105°F by midday. All outdoor time is either before 10am or after 6pm. Rideshare freely — this is the smart call, not a compromise.
♡
Soaking tub: The Palazzo suite has a deep tub. Use it every evening — excellent for leg and back relief over 5 nights.
♡
Nearest hospital: Sunrise Hospital & Medical Center, 3186 S Maryland Pkwy — ~10 min from the Strip. Store the address. Just smart to have.
💡 Things Most People Miss
💡
Mott 32 Peking duck: Pre-order when you book — 24 hrs required, carved tableside. The best Peking duck in Vegas. Don't skip this.
💡
Mob Museum speakeasy: In the basement — a Prohibition-era speakeasy bar. Great mocktail selection for Harriet. Worth 20 minutes after the exhibits.
💡
Shark Reef jellyfish room: Most visitors rush through it. The jellyfish exhibit is lit beautifully and deserves 15 minutes of just standing and watching — genuinely hypnotic.
💡
Venetian gondola — mornings are quietest: If you do it on Day 6 Sunday morning, it's the most peaceful version of the ride. Weekday afternoons are also good. Avoid Saturday evenings.
💡
Peter Luger — Vegas takes cards: The Brooklyn original is cash-only. Caesars Palace location accepts all major credit cards. No ATM needed.
💡
Bellagio bridge for fountains: The bridge that spans the lake gives the straight-on view of the entire fountain spread. Arrive 5 min early on Saturdays — the 8pm Bocelli show draws a crowd.
